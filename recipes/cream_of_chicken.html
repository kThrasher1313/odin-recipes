Cream of Chicken Soup

About 2 cans worth

Prep: 5 min, Cook: 10-15 min

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter (45g)
  • 6 tbsp all purpose flour (45g)
  • 2 cups chicken stock (480g)
  • 1 cup milk (240g)
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream (60g)
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt (4g)
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper (1g)
  • 3/4 tsp garlic powder(2g)
  • 3/4 tsp onion powder (2g)
  • 1/2 tsp paprika (1g)
  • 3/4 cup cooked and finely chopped or shredded chicken (100 g)
  • Optional 1 tbsp chicken bouillion paste or powder

Steps

  1. Add butter to a medium pot over medium heat. Once melted, whisk in flour until smooth. Cook 2 to 3 minutes, whisking, until it smells nutty but is not browned.
  2. Slowly pour in the chicken stock while whisking so no lumps form. Add the milk and heavy cream, whisking until everything is smooth.
  3. Stir in salt, pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, and bouillon if using. Bring to a gentle simmer
  4. Simmer 4 to 7 minutes, stirring often, unitl the mixture is thick and glossy. If it coats the back of the spoon and a finger run through it leaves a clear line, you nailed it.
  5. Stir in the cooked, finely chopped or shredded chicken. Simmer another 2 to 3 minutes to warm it through.
  6. Taste and add a little more salt if needed. If it is too thick, whisk in more stock 15 to 30 g (1 to 2 tbsp) at a time. If it is too thin, let it simmer a few minutes longer

Tips

  • For casseroles, keep it slightly thicker so it behaves like the condensed canned version
  • For eating straight as soup, thin with extra warm stock until it hits your desired spoonable consistency
  • Use good quality stock or homemade broth, since that is where most of the flavor comes from
